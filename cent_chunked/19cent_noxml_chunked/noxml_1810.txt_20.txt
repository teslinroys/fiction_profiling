Frederic and Mary seem likely, in their retirement, to experience a far greater
degree of happiness than before his error: he now never languishes for change or
emancipation; but thinks himself only unhappy when bereft of the company of his
beloved wife, from whom he never experiences the slightest reproach, but who shews
him the sweetest and kindest attention, possesses thereby his fondest regard, while they
enjoy
"An elegant sufficiency, content,
"Retirement, rural quiet, friendship, books,
"- - - - - - - - useful life,
"Progressive virtue, and approving Heav'n!"
